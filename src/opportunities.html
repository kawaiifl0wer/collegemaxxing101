Internships Opportunities

STEM

Space / Aerospace

  • NASA STEM Internships – Work with engineers and scientists on projects in robotics, aerospace engineering, coding, and space science.
  • NASA GeneLab for High Schools – Analyze biological and genetic data from space experiments and learn bioinformatics used in space research.

Biology/Medicine

  • National Institutes of Health High School Summer Internship Program (HS-SIP) – Conduct biomedical research in NIH labs with scientists studying topics like genetics, neuroscience, and disease.
  • Governor's School for Integration of Biological & Statistical Sciences (IB&SS) - Hosted at ETSU, this five-week program integrates biology and statistics through coursework, guest speakers, and applied projects, earning students 7 college credits across three courses.

Chemistry

  • American Chemical Society Project SEED – Paid summer internships where students gain hands-on experience doing chemistry research in university or industry labs.

Computer/Data Science

  • NetSci High Research Program – Work with university researchers on network science projects involving coding, data analysis, and scientific presentations.
  • Governor's School for Emerging Technologies - Hosted at the University of Memphis, this program focuses on emerging technology fields.

Engineering/General STEM

  • Massachusetts Institute of Technology MITES Summer Program – Free, highly selective program with courses and projects in engineering, math, and computer science.
  • Governor's School for Science and Engineering (GSSE) - Held at UT Knoxville since 1985, this four-week program provides advanced study, lab experiences, and research in science and mathematics, supplemented by guest lectures, department tours, and field trips. UTK Students earn 5 college credits.
  • Governor's School for Computational Physics - Hosted at Austin Peay State University, this program combines physics, computer modeling, and research.
  • eVol10 - Hosted at UT Knoxville, thirty-two students will be selected to participate. Students will learn about careers in engineering, explore the campus, tour engineering labs and facilities, attend ACT preparation, and spend a week living on a college campus.
  • Lumiere Education - The Lumiere Research Scholar Program is a research-focused opportunity designed for high school students who want to explore academic topics in depth. You work 1-on-1 with a Ph.D. mentor on an independent project in a subject area you choose. Over the course of twelve weeks, you learn how to form a research question, gather information, and develop your ideas into a full research paper.
  • Oak Ridge National Lab NGSI - Next Generation STEM Internship Program (NGSI) at Oak Ridge National Laboratory is a paid, full-time summer internship for high school students who want real research exposure. Over seven weeks, you will work with scientists and engineers to contribute to ongoing projects. You will be matched with mentors based on interest and background, and the program includes lab tours, seminars, and poster sessions.

Environmental Science

  • Conservation Legacy Youth Conservation Corps Programs – Participate in hands-on environmental science projects like conservation work and ecological research.
  • Governor's School for the Agricultural Sciences (TGSAS) - Hosted at UT Martin, this program exposes students to agricultural business, veterinary science, meteorology, crop and soil management, agricultural engineering, and farm and ranch management.
  • Governor's School for Tennessee History and Environmental Studies - A four-week program at ETSU that explores the unique history and culture of Tennessee Students are able to complete three semester hours of college credit in Tennessee history, as well as an intensive, non-credit seminar in research, writing, and presentation skills.

Humanities

Public Policy & Law

  • Manhattan District Attorney’s High School Internship Program – Criminal justice, civic engagement, leadership, and participation in workshops and mock trials.
  • Brooklyn District Attorney’s Office High School Internship – High schoolers assist attorneys, attend real courtroom activities, and engage in community outreach.
  • New York State Senate / USSYP – Work with a state senator on legal/public policy, communications, and research projects.
  • Governor's School for the Humanities - Hosted at UT Martin, this program covers philosophy, literature, history, and culture, earning students 3 college credit hours.
  • Governor's School for International Studies (GSIS) - Hosted at UT Memphis, this four-week program immerses students in global cultures, languages, and perspectives, including six hours of college coursework.

Writing & Journalism

  • Princeton University Summer Journalism Program – Journalism experience for high school seniors interested in reporting, writing, and media, hosted at Princeton University.
  • StandOut Connect (Virtual Internships) – Matches high school students with virtual internships across fields, including law, writing, policy, and research.
  • Sewanee Young Writers' Conference (SYWC) - A two-week summer conference for high schoolers passionate about creative writing, held at the University of the South on the Cumberland Plateau in Sewanee, TN. The program covers five genres: fiction, poetry, songwriting, literary nonfiction, and fantasy, with rigorous workshops and one-on-one sessions with faculty.
  • Tennessee High School Press Association (THSPA) - Fall Workshop at UT Knoxville THSPA holds fall journalism workshops at multiple Tennessee locations, including one at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville every October. The association also holds an annual Awards Day competition with categories recognizing outstanding student journalism work.

Art

  • Smithsonian Internships for High School Students – Paid and unpaid internships in art curation, archives, conservation, and museum education.
  • The Met High School Internship Program – A paid opportunity for NYC, NJ, and CT students to work with creative professionals at The Metropolitan Museum of Art.
  • Governor's School for the Arts – Hosted at MTSU, this is a three-week intensive for students skilled in music, ballet/dance, theatre, filmmaking, and visual art. Students are selected through competitive auditions.
  • Arrowmont School of Arts and Crafts - Offers workshops in painting, woodworking, drawing, glass, photography, ceramics, fiber arts, book arts, and metalworking.
  • High School Art Academy (HSAA) - An all-day workshop experience for Tennessee high school students (grades 9–12) held at UT Knoxville's Art + Architecture Building. It's organized by the School of Art with support from Knox County art educators, and workshops are taught by UT faculty, grad students, and alumni.

Business/Finance

  • Governor's School For Technological Innovation and Business Leadership (GSTIBL) - Hosted at Tennessee Tech, this 28-day program covers entrepreneurship, business plan writing, and organizational management. Students form teams to build and pitch a business, earning 3 college credit hours.
  • Women Empowered Through Business (WEB) Institute - Women Empowered Through Business (WEB) Institute at the University of Tennessee is a free, residential program for rising junior girls interested in business, technology, and leadership. You will live on campus and attend workshops on coding, analytics, entrepreneurship, and supply chain concepts, alongside ACT prep and sessions on personal branding.
  • Business Education for Talented Students (BETS) - Business Education for Talented Students (BETS) is a free, ten-day summer program hosted by UT Knoxville’s Haslam College of Business for rising seniors. You will take part in workshops covering accounting, finance, marketing, entrepreneurship, and supply chain management taught by business faculty. Much of the learning happens through a multi-day marketplace simulation where you apply concepts as a team.
